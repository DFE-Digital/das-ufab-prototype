What is Co-investment?
Co-investment is when employers share the cost of training and assessing apprentices with the government.
You qualify for Co-investment, that means 95% of your training costs are paid by DFE.
As a levy paying employer you can access your levy funds through the apprenticeship service.
These funds in your apprenticeship account are available to spend on apprenticeship training in England.



Back

Using your apprenticeship levy

As an employer with a pay bill of more than £3 million you can manage funds using the apprenticeship service and spend it on training and assessing your apprentices.
The government will apply a 10% top up to the funds you have in your account.

If you don’t have enough funds, you just pay 5% of the outstanding balance and the government will pay the rest. This is up to the funding band maximum allocated to each specific apprenticeship.

If you exceed the funding band maximum, you’ll need to pay all the additional costs. If you don’t already have an account, find out how to set up an apprenticeship service account as an employer.
